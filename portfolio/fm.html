UI Design
Problem Statement
Feedlot managers rely on manual logs and disconnected tools to track cattle movements, feeding, and health. This leads to data loss, delayed updates, and poor communication, especially in areas with limited connectivity ultimately affecting efficiency, accuracy, and livestock performance.
Possible Solution
Feedlot Manager is a mobile application designed to streamline daily feedlot operations. It provides real-time tracking of cattle movements, feed, and health treatments, along with performance analytics to support data-driven decisions. Built for multi-user access, the app ensures seamless collaboration and reliability even in remote field conditions.
Target Audience
  • Feedlot Managers / Supervisors
  • Farm Owners / Livestock Producers
  • Ranch Hands / Field Staff
The Approach
A user-centered, field-focused approach guided the design process observing real feedlot workflows to address on-site challenges and offline needs. The result is a simple, mobile-first solution refined through user testing to ensure speed, reliability, and ease of use in demanding field conditions.

Empathize Phase
Qualitative Research
To understand the daily workflows, challenges, and motivations of feedlot managers and staff working in remote, data-driven environments.
Interview Questions
  • How do you currently track cattle movements, feed, and health treatments?
  • What are the most time-consuming or frustrating parts of your daily routine?
  • How often do you experience connectivity issues while working on-site?
  • What kind of data or reports do you wish you could access more easily?
  • How do you collaborate or share updates with your team?
  • What would make a digital tool easy and practical to use in your environment?
Key Insight Derived
  • Most managers still rely on paper logs or Excel sheets, making data hard to update or share.
  • Users value speed and simplicity over complex features.
  • Health and feed tracking are the most critical daily tasks.
  • A mobile app must have large touch targets and minimal typing for field use.
  • Managers are open to digital tools if they fit naturally into existing workflows.
Quantitative Research
To validate findings from interviews, a short survey was conducted with 15 feedlot managers and staff across different operations. The goal was to quantify adoption challenges, feature priorities, and connectivity limitations.

Brief Story
Tom manages daily operations at a mid-sized feedlot, leading a team of ranch hands and ensuring cattle are well-fed and healthy. He spends most of his day outdoors, juggling supervision and record-keeping often struggling with paperwork and poor connectivity.
Goals
  • Track daily movements and feeding easily.
  • Access accurate data anytime, anywhere.
  • Optimize feed costs and cattle performance.
Frustrations
  • Paperwork is time consuming.
  • Difficult to update or share data in real time.
  • Limited mobile friendly tools.
Needs
  • A simple, mobile first tool that works seamlessly offline.
  • Quick data entry options suited for outdoor environments.
  • Clear visibility into feed, health, and performance data.
  • A way to share updates instantly with his team.
Motivation
  • Reduce time spent on manual paperwork.
  • Improve accuracy and organization of cattle records.
  • Ensure team coordination and real-time communication.
  • Increase productivity with a tool that works anywhere, even offline.
